How To

There are two main types of home composting: Hot composting and Vermicomposting

Hot composting uses aerobic decomposition to actively decompose organic material.

Vermicomposting uses worms to decompose organic material.

Hot Composting

1. Gather materials.
Get green materials (lush organics) and brown materials (dry organics)

Grass clippings are green materials

2. Start layering your pile.
Start with a base layer of large brown materials for airflow
Keeping alternating between layers for balance
Water each layer, wanting the pile to feel like a wet sponge.

A good pile of green and brown materials

3. Monitor your pile.
Turn the pile every 1-2 weeks to aerate it
Check the temperature, wanting it to be between 130°F and 160°F

A thermometer for hot composting

4. Keep your pile wet.
Water the pile regularly to making sure its damp, not soggy
If the pile smells, it's too wet. Add more carbon material to balance.

A thermometer for hot composting

5. Wait for the compost to mature.
After 2-3 months, the compost should be ready to use
It should be dark, crumbly, and smell earthy

Finished hot compost

Vermicomposting

1. Gather materials.
Get red wiggler worms, a bin, and bedding material
(like shredded paper or coconut coir)

Red wiggler worms are the best for vermicomposting

2. Prepare the bin.
Drill holes in the bin for airflow
Add a layer of bedding material to the bottom

A vermicomposting bin with holes for airflow

3. Add food scraps gradually.
Add small amounts of vegetable and fruit scraps into the bin.
Avoid dairy and meat to stop odors and pests.

Food scraps are the food source for the worms.

4. Monitor moisture and aeration.
Ensure that the bedding remains moist but not waterlogged.
Turn or fluff the bedding periodically for proper aeration.

Good moisture helps keep worms healthy.

5. Harvest the compost.
After a few months, the finished compost will separate from the worm castings.
Remove the matured compost from the bin and use it to enrich garden soil.

Harvested vermicompost.

These are the two most popular home compost methods.
